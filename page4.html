How to get there?

By Bus

If you want to commute from Manila to Patar Beach, you can ride a bus that goes to Bolinao, Pangasinan. You may proceed to the following bus terminals and ride any of these buses:

  • You can take a bus from Manila to Alaminos City, Pangasinan. Several bus companies operate routes to Alaminos City from different terminals in Manila, such as Victory Liner, Five Star Bus, and Dagupan Bus.
  • Board a bus bound for Alaminos City, Pangasinan. Make sure to inform the conductor or driver that you're heading to Bolinao.
  • Upon arriving at Alaminos City, take a tricycle or jeepney to the Bolinao town proper.
    •

    Regular or non-air-conditioned buses: Approximately PHP 350 to PHP 500 per person. Air-conditioned buses: Approximately PHP 400 to PHP 700 per person.


    By Car

    Take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) from Manila and head northbound towards the provinces of Bulacan, Pampanga, and Tarlac.


    Continue on the NLEX until you reach the exit for the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX).


    Take SCTEX and exit at the Tarlac City Exit, then follow signs leading to the town of Camiling.


    From Camiling, follow the road to Alaminos City, Pangasinan.


    Upon reaching Alaminos City, follow signs to Bolinao town proper.


    From Bolinao town proper, you can drive or ride a tricycle to Patar Beach.


    Travel tips


    • Research the best time to visit Patar Beach, considering weather conditions, crowd levels, and local events.
    • Make accommodation reservations in advance, especially during peak seasons, to secure your preferred lodging options.
    • Bring sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, and beachwear to protect yourself from the sun's rays.
    • Pack towels, swimsuits, and sandals for beach activities.
    • Decide on your mode of transportation to Patar Beach, whether by bus, private vehicle, or rental car.
    • If taking public transportation, familiarize yourself with bus schedules and routes to Bolinao from Manila or nearby cities.
    • Choose accommodation options that suit your preferences and budget, such as beachfront resorts, guesthouses, or homestays.
    • Swim only in designated areas and follow beach safety guidelines.

    About Patar Beach

    History of Patar Beach

    Bolinao area was inhabited by indigenous tribes, particularly the Pangasinense people. These indigenous communities had established settlements along the coast and engaged in fishing, farming, and trade.

    With increased tourism comes the importance of environmental conservation and sustainable management of natural resources. Efforts have been made to preserve the ecological integrity of Patar Beach and its surrounding marine ecosystems through initiatives such as beach clean-up drives, marine biodiversity conservation programs, and responsible tourism practices.
