How to get there?

By Bus

One common way is to take a bus from Manila to Bolinao. You can choose to ride either Five Star bus or Victory Liner from Cubao or Pasay.

You can ride a bus from Cubao or Pasay bound for Bolinao. Two popular bus liners that offer trips to Bolinao are Solid North and Five Star Bus Liners.

Buses usually depart every hour for Bolinao, but it's recommended to depart early in the morning, around 2 or 3 AM, to maximize your time exploring the place.

By Car

From Manila, you can take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) route. From SCTEX, take the Camiling exit and follow the signs to Bolinao. The drive will take approximately 5-6 hours depending on traffic conditions.


Travel tips


  • Research and plan your visit in advance. Check the operating hours, entrance fees, and any specific guidelines or requirements for visiting Bolinao Falls. This will help you prepare and make the most of your time there.
  • Bring essential items such as sunscreen, insect repellent, a hat, and comfortable footwear for walking and exploring. Don't forget to bring drinking water and snacks, as there may be limited food options available near the falls.
  • Dress comfortably and wear appropriate clothing for swimming or engaging in water activities if you plan to do so. It's also a good idea to bring a change of clothes and a towel
  • Always prioritize safety. Pay attention to warning signs, follow instructions from local guides or authorities, and be cautious when walking on slippery surfaces near the falls. If you're not a strong swimmer or unfamiliar with cliff diving, it's best to avoid those activities or seek guidance from experienced individuals.
  • Help preserve the natural beauty of Bolinao Falls by practicing responsible tourism. Avoid littering and dispose of trash properly. Respect the flora and fauna, and avoid touching or damaging any natural formations.
  • Consider hiring a local guide who can provide valuable insights and ensure your safety during your visit. They can also share interesting stories and information about the falls.
  • Bring a camera or smartphone to capture the beautiful scenery and moments during your visit. However, be mindful not to disturb other visitors or compromise safety while taking photos
  • Before your trip, check for any updates or changes in travel guidelines, especially during the current situation. Stay informed about any restrictions, safety protocols, or closures that may affect your visit to Bolinao Falls.

About Bolinao Falls

History of Bolinao Falls

Bolinao Falls is a natural beauty located in Bolinao, Pangasinan, Philippines. While specific historical information about Bolinao Falls is limited, it is believed that the falls were formed by the flowing waters from the rivers of elevated lands, eventually cascading down to a lower elevation and creating the natural wide basin below. The name "Bolinao Falls" is derived from the town's name, Bolinao, and the falls themselves.

In addition to its natural beauty, Bolinao is known for its pristine beaches, stunning rock formations, historical church, lighthouse, river, campsites, and waterfalls. Bolinao Falls 1, 2, and 3 are clusters of beautiful waterfalls located in Barangay Samang Norte, Bolinao, Pangasinan.
