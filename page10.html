How to get there?

By Bus

Take a bus to Urdaneta from your location. From Urdaneta, take a UV Express van or a tricycle to Tayug. In Tayug, take a tricycle to Sunflower Eco Park. It is recommended to ask the tricycle driver to wait for you since it may be difficult to find a tricycle for the return journey. The tricycle fare is approximately P150 each way.



By Car

Head towards Tayug, Pangasinan from your location.

Follow the route to Asingan-Urdaneta Road.

Turn onto Villasis-Asingan Road.

Take a slight left onto Lichauco-Tayug Road.

Continue driving until you reach Tayug Sunflower

Eco Park, which is located at Purok 4, Brgy. C-Lichauco, Tayug, Pangasinan.


Travel tips


  • Determine the mode of transportation you will be using. If you're traveling by bus, take a bus to Urdaneta and then transfer to a UV Express van or tricycle to Tayug. If you're driving, follow the directions to Tayug Sunflower Eco Park provided earlier. Consider using a GPS navigation system or a mobile navigation app for precise directions.
  • Check the opening hours and best time to visit Tayug Sunflower Eco Park. Sunflowers are typically in bloom during the summer months, but it's best to confirm the flowering season to ensure you can see the sunflowers in full bloom.
  • Inquire about the entrance fees and any additional charges for activities or amenities at Tayug Sunflower Eco Park. Prepare the necessary cash for your admission.
  • Tayug Sunflower Eco Park is a popular spot for photography. Bring your camera or smartphone and capture the beauty of the sunflowers and the surroundings. Remember to be respectful of the flowers and follow any guidelines set by the park.
  • As you'll be spending time in open areas, it's important to protect yourself from the sun. Wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses to shield yourself from the sun's rays. Consider bringing an umbrella for additional shade.
  • Dress comfortably and wear appropriate footwear for walking around the park. You may be exploring the sunflower maze or strolling through the fields, so choose clothing that allows freedom of movement.
  • Bring a water bottle to stay hydrated during your visit. You may also want to pack some snacks for energy during your time at the park, as there may be limited food options available.
  • Consider hiring a local guide to enhance your experience and gain more insights into the sunflowers and the park. They can provide valuable information and help you navigate the area.

About Tayug Sunflower Eco Park

History of Tayug Sunflower Eco Park

The Tayug Sunflower Eco Park, located in Tayug, Pangasinan, is a relatively new attraction established in 2017. The park features a sunflower maze that spans a significant area within a demonstration farm. The maze was designed by landscape artist Toni Rivera and consists of thousands of sunflower plants.

The park quickly gained popularity due to its stunning sunflower maze, which offers visitors the opportunity to immerse themselves in a sea of vibrant sunflowers. The maze's design resembles a fully opened sunflower, providing a unique and picturesque experience for visitors.
