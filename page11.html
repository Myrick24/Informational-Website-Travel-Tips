How to get there?

By Bus

From Manila, go to either Cubao or Pasay, where you can find bus terminals that offer trips to Bolinao. Victory Liner is one of the bus companies that operate routes to Bolinao from Cubao.

Purchase a bus ticket bound for Bolinao. Make sure to check the schedule and departure times beforehand.

Board the bus and enjoy the scenic journey to Bolinao. The travel time is approximately 6.5 hours, but it may vary depending on traffic conditions and stops along the way.



By Car

Use a navigation app or a GPS device to get the best driving directions from your location to Cape Bolinao. You can input "Cape Bolinao Lighthouse" as your destination.

Typically, the route involves driving north from Manila on the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) until you reach the Tarlac-Pangasinan-La Union Expressway (TPLEX).

Continue driving on the TPLEX until you reach the Rosales Exit. From there, follow the signs leading to Bolinao.

Once you arrive in Bolinao, you can ask for directions to Cape Bolinao or the Cape Bolinao Lighthouse. The locals will be able to guide you to the lighthouse.


Travel tips


  • Research and plan your trip in advance. Familiarize yourself with the location, attractions, and activities in Cape Bolinao. Check the weather forecast to choose the best time to visit.
  • Determine your mode of transportation. If you're traveling by bus, check the bus schedules and book your tickets in advance. If you're driving, ensure your vehicle is in good condition for the journey.
  • Book your accommodation in advance, especially during peak seasons. There are various resorts, hotels, and guesthouses in Bolinao that offer accommodations to suit different budgets and preferences.
  • Pack essential items for your trip, including comfortable clothing, swimwear, sunscreen, hats, sunglasses, insect repellent, and a first aid kit.
  • Bring enough cash as there may be limited ATMs or banking facilities in the area. It's advisable to have small bills for easier transactions.
  • Cape Bolinao is a natural attraction, so it's important to respect and preserve the environment. Avoid littering, follow designated paths, and be mindful of the flora and fauna.
  • Protect yourself from the sun's rays by wearing sunscreen, hats, and sunglasses. Consider bringing an umbrella or seeking shade during the hottest hours of the day.
  • Bring a water bottle and stay hydrated throughout your visit. It's essential to drink plenty of water, especially in a tropical climate.

About Cape Bolinao Lighthouse

History of Cape Bolinao Lighthouse

The Cape Bolinao Lighthouse was built in 1905 by a collaboration of American, British, and Filipino engineers. It was constructed to guide ships and seafarers safely to shore, helping to prevent sea disasters in the area.

The lighthouse showcases a combination of architectural influences from the American, British, and Filipino engineers involved in its construction. It is situated at an elevation of 107 meters (351 feet) above sea level, making it the second-highest lighthouse in the Philippines after the Cape Bojeador Lighthouse.
