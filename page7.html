Lingayen Beach

Lingayen Beach is a popular tourist destination located in Lingayen, Pangasinan in the Philippines. It is known for its golden sand, clear waters, and picturesque sunsets. The beach is a favorite spot for swimming, sunbathing, and picnicking.

How to get there?

By Bus

You can take a bus from Victory Liner or Five Star bus companies in Manila bound for Dagupan City. From Dagupan City, you can take a local bus or jeepney to Lingayen. The bus ride will take around 5-6 hours.

By Car

From Manila, take the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX) and continue on the Subic-Clark-Tarlac Expressway (SCTEX) until you reach Tarlac City. From there, take the MacArthur Highway and head north towards Lingayen. The drive will take approximately 4-5 hours, depending on traffic conditions.


Travel tips


  • Bring appropriate beach essentials such as sunscreen, towels, hats, and sunglasses to protect yourself from the sun.
  • Stay hydrated by bringing plenty of water and snacks to keep yourself energized throughout the day
  • Wear appropriate beach attire such as swimwear and flip-flops for a comfortable beach experience
  • Respect the beach rules and regulations set by the local authorities to maintain the cleanliness and safety of the beach.
  • Be mindful of your surroundings and keep an eye on your belongings to prevent any theft or loss.
  • Take care of the environment by disposing of your trash properly and avoiding any littering on the beach.
  • Enjoy the beauty of Lingayen Beach by taking in the scenic views and participating in beach activities such as swimming, beach volleyball, or simply relaxing by the shore.
  • Be cautious of strong currents and waves, especially if you are not a strong swimmer. Always swim in designated areas with a lifeguard on duty..

About Lingayen

History of Lingayen Beach

The town of Lingayen was founded in 1614 by Augustinian missionaries and Spanish conquistadores. It is named after a corpulent tamarind tree that grew on the present town plaza at that time.

Lingayen Beach holds historical significance as it was where the Chinese warlord Limahong, also known as Ah Hong, escaped to Northern Luzon in 1574. He dug a channel in Domalandan for his escape to the China Sea
